How It Works

14-week semester,
updated weekly

Each week, every student agent processes attendance, assignments, stress, knowledge, motivation, and fatigue — then peer learning and interventions run.

1

Attendance Draw

Bernoulli probability modulated by stress, motivation, schedule, and room temperature.

2

Assignment Outcomes

Each assignment: on-time, late, or missed — weighted by stress and motivation.

3

Stress Update

Driven by workload, pending feedback, absence. Reduced by rest, SES buffer, and grit.

4

Knowledge Update

Topic-aware learning with prerequisite dependency, difficulty curve, fatigue penalty, and mid-semester slump.

5

Peer Learning + Intervention

Heterogeneous study groups transfer knowledge via ZPD. Targeted tutoring boosts bottom quartile.

# Each of 14 weeks, every student is updated for week in range(1, 15): for student in active_students: # 1. Does the student show up? attended = draw_attendance(student) # ↑ probability modulated by stress, motivation, # schedule, and room temperature # 2. Assignments: on-time / late / missed submitted, late, missed = draw_assignments(student) # 3. Stress accumulates from workload + absences # and drops with rest + feedback + SES buffer student.stress = update_stress(student, attended, received_feedback=week >= feedback_delay) # 4. Knowledge: topic-aware learning # Δk = rate × attended × effectiveness × capacity # × motivation × (1 − stress) × difficulty × slump student.knowledge = update_knowledge(student, topic) # 5. Motivation, fatigue, satisfaction student.motivation = update_motivation(student, feedback) student.fatigue = update_fatigue(student, attended) # 6. Peer learning: knowledge transfers # from strongest group member via ZPD apply_peer_learning(student, study_group) # ── End of semester ────────────────────────── # GPA = 0.7 × knowledge + 0.3 × task completion # scaled to 0–4, with late penalty from strictness gpa = compute_gpa(student) # clamped [0, 4]
